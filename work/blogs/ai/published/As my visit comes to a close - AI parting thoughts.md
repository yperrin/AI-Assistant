### A Word on Our Tools
**GitHub Copilot** is not the best AI tool for coding, let’s start with that. But at the level we are at, until we start running multi-agents AI orchestrated workflows, we are good. We are so far from this level of sophistication that by the time we get there, GitHub Copilot will probably have feature parity. This field works really fast. Let’s focus on learning what we have before we respond to internet Fads without knowing exactly what. At 40% adoption, GitHub Copilot is the most popular AI tool at the moment.

### Removing Roadblocks
One of my focuses is to identify all the manual processes we have currently. If we increase our ability to create code (the current bottleneck), any manual process will now become a blocker and restrict our ability to produce more. As an example, think about all the meetings we have.

**Current Meeting Volume:**
* **Average Weekly Time:** Most professionals spend roughly 12 hours in meetings per week.
* **Manager & Executive Burden:** This increases to 16 hours for people managers and 23 hours for executives.
* **Historical Growth:** Meeting frequency has tripled since 2020.

The purpose of a meeting should be to make decisions; communication (the many reports we create) should be automated. Our default behavior should be for us to code and only meet when decisions are necessary (requirements review).

Also, if we go back to the **Mythical Man Month**: The size of a team dictates the amount of communication the team needs to go through. In the age of AI, a 5-person team (SA, QA, developers, PO) would be the ideal, most productive team.

### The Vision for Efficiency
Finally, I want to address the question about what happens when we reach this level of efficiency. In general, companies think about this in two ways:
1.  **Workforce Reduction:** Reducing overhead to maintain the same output.
2.  **Expansion:** Using newfound efficiency to address sidelined projects and create new offerings.

The strength of Clarivate is its data, but it is also its people with the critical thinking and domain knowledge that allow us to make the right decisions in a world where writing code has very little cost.

**The Core Philosophy: Jevons Paradox**
Rather than viewing AI as a tool to cut headcount, the **Jevons Paradox** suggests a different approach: when a resource (intelligence/execution) becomes cheaper and more efficient, total consumption actually increases. I would like to suggest that the demand for judgment, creativity, and domain expertise is about to explode because previously "unviable" projects are now profitable.
