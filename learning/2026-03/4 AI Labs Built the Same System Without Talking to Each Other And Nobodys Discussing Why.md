This video challenges the prevailing assumption that AI capabilities are inherently **"jagged"**—brilliant at some tasks but terrible at others. The narrator argues that this jaggedness was actually a **deployment problem** resulting from asking AI to solve complex problems in a single, unorganized turn, rather than a limitation of its intelligence (0:00-1:20).The video outlines a major shift towards **"inference computing"** and **multi-agent orchestration**, where AI systems are placed within **"harnesses"** (scaffolding and structure) to manage long-horizon work. This allows the AI to plan, attempt tasks, fail, learn, and retry, much like a human professional team (1:45-3:20).**Key insights and examples include:**

- **Cursor's Breakthrough:** (7:00) A coding agent platform successfully solved a research-grade mathematics problem (*spectral graph theory*) by applying a **hierarchical planner-worker-judge architecture** originally designed for software engineering.

- **Four-Company Convergence:** (13:50) Major labs (Anthropic, DeepMind, OpenAI, and Cursor) independently developed similar agentic structures based on **decomposition, parallelization, and verification**.

- **The New Skillset:** (22:00) As execution becomes cheaper, the competitive advantage for knowledge workers shifts to **"sniff-checking"**—developing the meta-skills to verify, evaluate, and direct AI output rather than just producing it.
