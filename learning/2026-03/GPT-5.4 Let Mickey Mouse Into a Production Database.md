### **Highs & Lows**

- **The "Car Wash" Failure:** In a viral test, GPT-5.4 suggested walking to a car wash 100 meters away, failing to realize you need the car there to wash it. Both [Claude](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-_vL1KXd2rc) and [Gemini](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-_vL1KXd2rc) correctly identified it as a trick [00:54 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-_vL1KXd2rc&t=54).

- **The "Mickey Mouse" Incident:** During an "eval from hell" involving migrating messy business data, GPT-5.4 successfully parsed 99% of files but lacked the "hygiene" to filter out fake test data like a customer named "Mickey Mouse" [08:16 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-_vL1KXd2rc&t=496).

- **Thinking vs. Auto Mode:** There is a massive performance chasm. In **Thinking Mode**, it is world-class at factual retrieval (e.g., Higgs Boson mass). In **Auto Mode**, it hallucinated 2024 Nobel winners for a 2025 question and ranked dead last [10:18 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-_vL1KXd2rc&t=618).

### **Key Strengths**

- **Quantitative Modeling:** Built superior statistical spreadsheets (e.g., NFL win probabilities) compared to Claude’s cleaner but shallower versions [14:12 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-_vL1KXd2rc&t=852).

- **File Processing:** Handled nearly every file type (OCR, JSON, Excel, corrupted backups) with 99.1% coverage, whereas Claude skipped files it couldn't immediately parse [16:08 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-_vL1KXd2rc&t=968).

- **Self-Awareness:** It is the only model that accurately understands its own capabilities and the current AI competitive landscape [11:18 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-_vL1KXd2rc&t=678).

### **Key Weaknesses**

- **Writing & Tone:** Still lacks the "editorial voice" and product management judgment of [Claude Opus 4.6](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-_vL1KXd2rc) [18:52 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-_vL1KXd2rc&t=1132).

- **Speed:** It is significantly slower for complex tasks, taking 56 minutes for an evaluation Claude finished in 15 [20:06 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-_vL1KXd2rc&t=1206).

### **Summary Verdict**

- Building complex agentic systems.

- Deep quantitative/analytical work.

- Processing massive, "shoe box" style data dumps.

- High-quality writing and executive communications.

- Product strategy and nuanced decision-making.

- Tasks requiring speed and immediate actionability.
