### The Core Problem

- **Invisible Intent**: Human intent (the "why") isn't explicitly labeled in code or caught in unit tests. It is woven into the nuances and trade-offs made during development.

- **Death by Optimization**: AI makes "perfectly reasonable" changes that improve functional metrics (speed, efficiency) but inadvertently scrape away the emotional layer or brand voice.

- **The Ship of Theseus**: As AI autonomously fixes bugs and pushes updates, a system eventually becomes functionally superior but "emotionally flatter," with no easy way to recover the original human vision.

### Three Habits to Prevent Erosion

1. **Encode Intent into the Product**: Don't just document requirements; write down the *why* (e.g., "This southern accent is for brand warmth") and store it directly inside the codebase where AI and future builders will find it.

1. **Put Intent in the Path of Builders**: Ensure that any person or AI making a change is forced to encounter the intent documentation before they start building.

1. **Lead with Purpose, Not Features**: When requesting changes, describe the desired impact and feeling rather than just technical specifications. The "why" is what guides small choices at the margins.
