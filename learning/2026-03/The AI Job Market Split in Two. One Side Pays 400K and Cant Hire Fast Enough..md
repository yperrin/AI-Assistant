### The 7 Critical AI Skills

1. **Specification Precision (Clarity of Intent):** Moving beyond simple "prompting" to writing precise, technical specifications that a machine can follow literally without needing to "read between the lines" [04:41 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4cuT-LKcmWs&t=281).

1. **Evaluation and Quality Judgment:** The most cited skill. It involves building automated "eval harnesses" and resisting the urge to trust AI just because it sounds "fluent" [07:01 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4cuT-LKcmWs&t=421).

1. **Task Decomposition and Delegation:** Treating AI as a multi-agent system. This is a managerial skill focused on breaking complex projects into manageable segments for "planner" and "sub-agent" workflows [10:12 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4cuT-LKcmWs&t=612).

1. **Failure Pattern Recognition:** Identifying specific AI failure modes like **context degradation**, **specification drift**, **sycophantic confirmation**, and **silent failures** [13:07 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4cuT-LKcmWs&t=787).

1. **Trust and Security Design:** Determining the "blast radius" of errors and deciding where to keep humans in the loop to authorize actions versus where agents can run autonomously [16:30 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4cuT-LKcmWs&t=990).

1. **Context Architecture:** Organizing company data so agents can find it easily (described as the "Dewey Decimal System for agents") to ensure they have the right information on demand [19:11 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4cuT-LKcmWs&t=1151).

1. **Cost and Token Economics:** Calculating the ROI and blended cost of tasks across different models to determine if building an agentic system is actually worth the investment [21:01 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4cuT-LKcmWs&t=1261).

### Key Takeaways for You

- **Target Roles:** These skills apply to AIPM, Engineering, Operations, and AI Reliability roles [23:10 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4cuT-LKcmWs&t=1390).

- **Accessibility:** Unlike the PC revolution of the 80s, the "barrier to entry" is low; these skills are learnable using the AI tools themselves [04:12 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4cuT-LKcmWs&t=252).

- **The "Split":** If you aren't in the top half of the "K," the market feels impossible; if you are, you can "write your own ticket" [03:32 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4cuT-LKcmWs&t=212).
