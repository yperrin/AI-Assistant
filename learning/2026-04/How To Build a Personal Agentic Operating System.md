## [How To Build a Personal Agentic Operating System](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ntvkDnk_5jA)

**The AI Daily Brief | Guest: Nufar Gaspar**

---

### **Introduction: The Shift from Tools to Systems**

Nufar Gaspar introduces "Agent OS," a framework for building a personal agentic operating system. As AI tools (Cursor, Claude Code, Windsurf, etc.) converge on similar capabilities, the specific tool matters less than the underlying system of text files that define who you are, what you know, and how you work.

### **The 7 Layers of the Agentic OS**

### **1. Identity: Who are you?** [08:09 Opens in a new window](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ntvkDnk_5jA&t=489)

The foundational layer that tells the tool who it is working for.

- **Purpose:** Enforces rules and communication styles (e.g., "be direct," "never flatter me").

- **Tip:** Don't write this from scratch. Let an AI interview you with 15 questions about your preferences to draft it.

### **2. Context: What do you know?** [11:08 Opens in a new window](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ntvkDnk_5jA&t=668)

Specific information about your world that generic models don't have.

- **Focus:** Maintain 3–5 focused files (team, product, roadmap, stakeholders) rather than one massive stale document.

- **Practice:** Every time you have to re-explain something to an AI, add it to a context file.

### **3. Skills: How do you work?** [13:50 Opens in a new window](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ntvkDnk_5jA&t=830)

Reusable instruction sets for repeatable workflows.

- **Examples:** Weekly status updates, meeting pre-reads, or stakeholder email drafting.

- **Formula:** "When I say [Trigger], do [Process] using [Sources] to produce [Output]."

### **4. Memory: What do you remember?** [15:35 Opens in a new window](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ntvkDnk_5jA&t=935)

Managing how the AI retains information across sessions.

- **Strategy:** While tools have built-in memory, you should be deliberate. Create specialized memory files for decision logs or relationship context.

- **Audit:** Periodically ask your tool, "Explain how your memory system works," to understand its current limits.

### **5. Connections: How do you act?** [18:59 Opens in a new window](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ntvkDnk_5jA&t=1139)

Enabling the agent to reach external systems (Email, Slack, Jira) via protocols like [MCP](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ntvkDnk_5jA) (Model Context Protocol).

- **Safety Rule:** Start with **read-only** access. Only grant write access after building trust over several weeks.

- **Risk Management:** Be wary of "gossiping agents" that might share private notes with colleagues if permissions are too loose.

### **6. Verification: How do you check?** [21:32 Opens in a new window](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ntvkDnk_5jA&t=1292)

The discipline of auditing AI output and the system itself.

- **Process:** Run 3–5 quick checks on high-stakes outputs.

- **Maintenance:** Audit the OS every 8 weeks to ensure context files and instructions haven't gone stale.

### **7. Automations: What runs while you sleep?** [23:31 Opens in a new window](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ntvkDnk_5jA&t=1411)

Agents running tasks without immediate human triggers.

- **Guardrails:** Only automate workflows you have already performed manually many times.

- **Logging:** Always ensure there are logs so you can review what the agent did while you were away.

---

### **Conclusion: The Compounding Return** [24:27 Opens in a new window](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ntvkDnk_5jA&t=1467)

Building the "OS" is the hard part, but once established, adding new agents (e.g., a "Research Agent" or "Board Prep Agent") becomes incredibly fast because they inherit the existing identity, context, and skills. The goal is to build a foundation that travels with you, regardless of which AI model or harness becomes popular next.
