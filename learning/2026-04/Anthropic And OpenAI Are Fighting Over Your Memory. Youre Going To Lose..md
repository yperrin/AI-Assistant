The video [Anthropic And OpenAI Are Fighting Over Your Memory. You're Going To Lose.](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4KAF72BTyCE) by Nate B Jones discusses the growing problem of **AI context fragmentation** and the "stickiness" created by proprietary memory systems in platforms like ChatGPT and Claude.

The core argument is that as you use an AI, you are building a valuable professional asset—your **working intelligence**—which currently resides in walled gardens that you don't own and cannot easily move.

---

### The 4 Layers of AI Context

Jones identifies four specific types of context that users unknowingly "encode" into their AI over time:

- **Domain Encoding ****[03:49 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4KAF72BTyCE&t=229)****:** Industry vocabulary, internal acronyms, and regulatory environments learned through hundreds of daily interactions.

- **Workflow Calibration ****[06:18 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4KAF72BTyCE&t=378)****:** Specific preferences for how you want research structured, code reviewed, or documents drafted.

- **Behavioral Relationship ****[07:25 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4KAF72BTyCE&t=445)****:** The emergent understanding of unstated preferences—when to be challenged versus when to just execute.

- **Artifact Layer ****[09:13 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4KAF72BTyCE&t=553)****:** The "encoded thinking" and rationale behind the documents or code you produce, which is currently buried in fragmented chat histories.

### The Problem: Platform Incentives

The video explains why this issue remains unsolved:

- **Lock-in ****[13:51 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4KAF72BTyCE&t=831)****:** Model makers (OpenAI, Anthropic, etc.) have no incentive to make memory portable; they want to keep users inside their specific ecosystem.

- **Corporate IT Friction ****[00:31 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4KAF72BTyCE&t=31)****:** IT departments often ban personal AI accounts, forcing workers to use "empty" corporate instances that lack the context that makes them productive.

- **Diffuse Pain ****[14:38 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4KAF72BTyCE&t=878)****:** The loss of context is a "candy product" problem rather than an "opium product" problem—it's a constant, dull annoyance rather than an acute, sudden crisis, so people rarely seek third-party solutions until they switch jobs or tools.

### The Solution: Bring Your Own Context (BYOC)

Jones proposes a shift toward **Personal Context Sovereignty**:

- **Markdown Files ****[17:01 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4KAF72BTyCE&t=1021)****:** A simple "band-aid" solution where you use a structured document to store your preferences and paste them into new AI sessions.

- **Model Context Protocol (MCP) ****[17:46 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4KAF72BTyCE&t=1066)****:** A more robust solution using an MCP-native memory store. This allows an AI to "pull" relevant context as needed rather than having it "pushed" in via long prompts.

- **Personal Databases ****[20:54 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4KAF72BTyCE&t=1254)****:** Moving from a static document to a persistent, evolvable database (like Postgress or Supabase) that you manage and control.

### Key Takeaway

In the AI age, your value as a professional now includes a **fifth category of capital**: your accumulated AI working intelligence [27:36 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4KAF72BTyCE&t=1656). To maintain a competitive advantage, you must treat this context as a portable asset that travels with you regardless of the platform or employer.
