### 1. Executive Summary

JPMorgan Chase stands as a pioneer in enterprise AI, having launched its internal **LLM Suite** nearly two and a half years ago—well before the mainstream surge of ChatGPT. The core of their strategy is the **"Agent Economy,"** a decentralized approach where AI tools are distributed directly to employees rather than being siloed in central IT.

**Key Takeaways:**

- **Connectivity Over Models:** JPMorgan treats LLMs as commodities. The true competitive advantage (their "moat") lies in how these models connect to the bank's internal data, trading systems, and specific business processes.

- **Decentralized Innovation:** By providing reusable "building blocks," the firm empowered employees to create over **30,000 personal assistants**, driving a viral adoption rate where over **60%** of the workforce now uses AI daily.

- **The Transformation Gap:** While bottom-up innovation has streamlined tasks, the next frontier is a top-down strategic rethink of long-term end-to-end processes (e.g., credit dispersal or employee onboarding) specifically for an AI-native world.

The following is a structured presentation of the conversation between the hosts and **Derek Waldron**.

**The Three Principles of JPMorgan’s AI Strategy**

> "We had three insights which became principles... One, we have to distribute the technology into the hands of the employee base at large... Second, no two job functions in a large enterprise are exactly the same... it can't be solutioned from the center... And the third was the realization that the actual long-term bottleneck... was not going to be about the model. It was going to be about how the technology connects into the technology estate and data and process estate." [01:54 Opens in a new window](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rLxJzeRGzV8&t=114)

**On the Viral Adoption & "Gamification"**

> "Over the course of those first few months there we went from zero users to 250,000 users... there was actually a little bit of a competitive angle to it when your neighbor has it and you don't. And I think that gamification was actually helpful to turbocharge the adoption." [00:00 Opens in a new window](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rLxJzeRGzV8&t=0)

**The Evolution of RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)**

> "We're actually in our fourth generation now of a RAG system... The first was a basic pipeline... The second was where we democratized it... federated to the entire firm... The third was realizing that not all knowledge is created equal... we needed a hierarchy of information... The fourth generation was much more around multimodality... reports with graphs or company pitch books." [16:34 Opens in a new window](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rLxJzeRGzV8&t=994)

**The "Agent Economy" & Personal Assistants**

> "A few months ago we launched the capability of personal assistants... we wanted to give them a little more power and convenience... We were surprised by just how viral it was because the organization interpreted that... they weren't just designing prompts. They were building assets. They were building assistance. People were sharing the assistance they built." [22:54 Opens in a new window](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rLxJzeRGzV8&t=1374)

**Governance and Safety Guardrails**

> "We call these personal assistants because these are for individual use only. But we do have the means by which then a great personal assistant can be created into more of a team or a firmwide solution... that goes through other types of governance mechanisms." [26:27 Opens in a new window](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rLxJzeRGzV8&t=1587)

**Future Vision: Top-Down Transformation**

> "Business runs on long processes... if we want to be able to really move the needle on those processes—think reducing the end-to-end time to disperse credit or onboarding a new employee—there has to be a little more of a strategic element to actually rethink what the process itself will need to look like in a world of AI." [43:30 Opens in a new window](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rLxJzeRGzV8&t=2610)
