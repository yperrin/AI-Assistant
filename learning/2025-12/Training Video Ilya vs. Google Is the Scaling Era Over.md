- **Ilya’s View:** The era of simply "scaling" (making models bigger with more data) to get better results is over. He argues that pre-training is a "blunt instrument" and that we are entering a new "Age of Research" where novel paradigms are needed [06:28 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DcrXHTOxi3I&t=388).

- **Google’s View:** Google (and other labs) arguably believe scaling is still working, citing models like Gemini 3 as proof that pre-training and post-training continue to yield results [04:01 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DcrXHTOxi3I&t=241).

- **"Smarter on Paper than in Practice":** Models score high on benchmarks ("genius") but can be brittle in real life ("useful idiot"). Ilya notes that humans are "reward hackers" who build tests models can game, rather than tests that prove true understanding [00:12 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DcrXHTOxi3I&t=12).

- **The Generalization Gap:** A "deep technical claim" is that AI generalizes far worse than humans. A teenager can learn to drive in ~10 hours; an AI needs the data equivalent of 10,000 hours. Ilya wants to solve for "sample efficiency"—learning more from less [02:30 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DcrXHTOxi3I&t=150).

- **Emotions as "Value Functions":** Ilya argues that emotions aren't just decorative; they are efficient "value functions" that help humans make decisions *before* an outcome occurs (e.g., a "gut feeling"), whereas Reinforcement Learning (RL) is inefficient because it only looks backward at the end of a task [04:47 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DcrXHTOxi3I&t=287).

- **Redefining AGI:** Instead of defining AGI as "a system that can do every job" (which no single human can do), Ilya defines it as a "super intelligent learner"—essentially a mind capable of learning any new task faster and deeper than a human [08:34 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DcrXHTOxi3I&t=514).

- **Safe Superintelligence (SSI) Strategy:** Ilya’s new company is "research-first" with no immediate consumer products. The goal is to solve the generalization problem first rather than just building a slightly better chatbot [07:46 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DcrXHTOxi3I&t=466).

- **Incremental Deployment:** Despite his focus on safety, Ilya argues for "incremental deployment." We cannot reason about a superintelligence we haven't built yet, so we must release increasingly powerful systems to learn how to manage them fundamentally [09:56 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DcrXHTOxi3I&t=596).
