- **The Problem:** While AI provides huge productivity gains for individual developers on specific tasks, most large enterprises are only seeing marginal improvements (5-15%) overall [03:28 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SZStlIhyTCY&t=208).

- **The Cause:** This disconnect is due to existing Agile models creating bottlenecks that can't handle the speed of AI. These include manual code reviews for massive code generation, slow human collaboration, and an increase in technical debt [04:28 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SZStlIhyTCY&t=268).

- **The Solution: AI-Native Operating Model:** Top-performing companies are **seven times more likely** to succeed by adopting AI-native workflows and roles [08:37 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SZStlIhyTCY&t=517):

	- **Workflows:** They are moving from quarterly planning to **continuous planning** and from story-driven to **spec-driven development**, where Product Managers (PMs) iterate on detailed specifications with agents [09:36 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SZStlIhyTCY&t=576).

	- **Team Structure:** They are shifting away from the 8-10 person "two-pizza" teams to smaller, cross-functional "one-pizza" pods (3-5 individuals) [09:59 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SZStlIhyTCY&t=599).

	- **Roles:** Engineers are becoming **orchestrators** who divide work between humans and agents, with consolidated roles like "product builders" managing agents and possessing full-stack fluency [10:07 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SZStlIhyTCY&t=607), [13:17 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SZStlIhyTCY&t=797).

- **Scaling Requires Change Management:** To scale these gains, organizations must implement a comprehensive change management approach that includes hands-on upskilling, new incentive structures, and a measurement system that prioritizes business outcomes (time-to-market, quality, cost reduction) over just tool adoption [15:58 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SZStlIhyTCY&t=958), [18:33 Opens in a new window ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SZStlIhyTCY&t=1113).
