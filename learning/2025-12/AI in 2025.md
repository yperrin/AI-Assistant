- **Early 2025: Reasoning and DeepSeek**

	- The year began with simple "tab-completion" tools where developers still manually integrated AI-generated code.

	- The release of [DeepSeek](https://www.deepseek.com/) shifted the industry's focus toward "test-time compute" and reasoning, proving that intelligence was more critical than model size.

- **February 2025: The "Vibe Coding" Era**

	- Andrew Karpathy introduced the concept of **["vibe coding"](https://www.google.com/search?q=https%3A%2F%2Fx.com%2Fkarpathy%2Fstatus%2F1886111534065934547)**, a high-level, iterative approach that allowed non-engineers to build software.

- **March 2025: Planning with Gemini**

	- The experimental [Gemini 2.5 Pro](https://www.google.com/search?q=https%3A%2F%2Fblog.google%2Ftechnology%2Fai%2Fgoogle-gemini-ai-update-december-2024%2F) model introduced the ability for AI to formulate and execute technical plans.

- **June 2025: The Agentic Unlock**

	- Anthropic's release of [Claude Code](https://www.google.com/search?q=https%3A%2F%2Fdocs.anthropic.com%2Fen%2Fdocs%2Fagents-and-tools%2Fclaude-code) marked a "watershed moment," providing an agentic harness that could work autonomously for hours.

	- This shift turned engineers into "bookends" who define requirements at the start and review the final output at the end.

- **Late 2025: System-Level Creation**

	- Newer models, including [Claude 4.5](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-3-5-sonnet) and [Gemini 3](https://www.google.com/search?q=https%3A%2F%2Fblog.google%2Ftechnology%2Fai%2Fgoogle-gemini-ai-update-december-2024%2F), demonstrated the ability to understand entire architectures rather than just isolated code snippets.

	- By the end of the year, developers could provide a dense requirements document (PRD) and receive a functional, multi-file system in less than an hour.
