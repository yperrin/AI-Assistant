- Easy (Proximity): AI makes it "easy" to generate code quickly, but this often involves copy-pasting solutions or adding layers without structural thought [04:37].

- Simple (Structure): Simplicity requires intentional design and untangling, which AI cannot do on its own [04:45].

- The Trap: Every time we choose "easy" via AI, we are trading immediate speed for future complexity. AI treats all existing patterns (including technical debt) as "correct" and reproduces them, leading to an "infinite" scale of generation that outpaces human comprehension [07:02].
The Solution: Three-Phase "Context Engineering"
Nations suggests a structured approach to prevent the "complexity spiral" [11:13]:

- Phase 1: Research (The Human Checkpoint) [11:25]

	- Feed the AI documentation, diagrams, and Slack threads.

	- Iteratively probe the AI's analysis to create a single "Research Document" that maps dependencies and effects.

- Phase 2: Detailed Implementation Planning [12:20]

	- Create a "paint-by-numbers" spec including function signatures and data flow.

	- This is where humans make the critical architectural decisions that AI might overlook.

- Phase 3: Implementation [13:20]

	- With a precise plan, the AI can generate clean, focused code in one shot, avoiding the "dead code" often created during long conversational iterations.
Key Takeaway
Nations emphasizes that understanding remains a human endeavor [18:06]. He recounts a Netflix refactor where they had to perform a manual migration first just to "earn the understanding" required to guide the AI safely [15:52]. The goal is to use AI to accelerate mechanical tasks without "outsourcing our thinking."
