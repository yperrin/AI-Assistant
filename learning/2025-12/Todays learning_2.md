### 1. Champion AI Adoption and Provide Direct Support

- **Act as an AI Advocate and Problem Solver:** Since many managers already field questions about using AI tools at least weekly, continue to **support your teams to become more adept AI users**. You should also **recommend AI tools** to your teams to solve problems.

- **Move Beyond Pilots:** Empower your team to move successful AI use cases from initial **pilot stages to scale**.

- **Listen to Employees:** **Listen to employees** describe how they are using AI today and how they envision their work being transformed. This allows you to better capture the value of AI.

- **Elevate Change Champions:** Especially in functions with high economic potential but lukewarm employee optimism (like sales, engineering, or R&D), consider **elevating change champions** who can improve employee sentiment and guide peers.

### 2. Prioritize Training and Skill Development

- **Provide Formal and On-the-Job Training:** Recognize that employees are not always getting the support they need. You must **provide employees with much-needed training** and meet employee needs with **on-the-job training** and human-centric development.

- **Tailor Training to Roles:** Upskilling is critical, and you should ensure training is tailored to specific roles. For instance, you could offer **prompt engineering classes** to specific functional teams.

- **Focus on Future Skills:** Instead of dwelling on the jobs expected to be displaced, you should plan for the projected **170 million new jobs** and the new skills those roles will require.

- **Offer Access and Incentives:** Provide access to AI tools, such as in the form of **betas or pilots**, and explore incentives like **financial rewards and recognition** to improve AI uptake.

### 3. Foster Trust and Human-Centricity

- **Ensure Transparent Communication:** Maintain **transparent communication with your teams** throughout the AI development process.

- **Involve Non-Technical Employees:** Advocate for the inclusion of nontechnical employees in the early development stages of AI tools, particularly during **ideation and requirement gathering**. This human-centric approach helps ensure fairness and impartiality in the systems.

- **Address Safety Concerns:** Be prepared to address top employee safety concerns, which include **cybersecurity, privacy, and accuracy**.

### 4. Support AI Talent and Culture

- **Cultivate an Attractive Environment:** Help create an environment that is attractive to technologists by providing them with time to **experiment**, offering **access to cutting-edge tools**, and promoting a **collaborative engineering culture**.

- **Commit to Ethical Benchmarks:** While the focus is often on immediate business outcomes, you should support the use of AI benchmarks, including **ethical ones**, to improve model transparency and accountability.
